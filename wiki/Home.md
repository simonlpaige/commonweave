# Commonweave Wiki

Welcome to the Commonweave project wiki. This is the working knowledge base for contributors, collaborators, and anyone trying to understand how the project is built and what it's trying to do.

---

## What is Commonweave?

Commonweave is an open directory of cooperative, mutual-aid, and commons-based organizations - mapped, searchable, and connected to a set of working notes on post-labor economic design.

The core question it tries to answer: **when machines do most of the work, who benefits?** Right now, whoever owns the machines. Commonweave documents the organizations around the world that are trying to answer that differently.

**The thesis in one sentence:** Selective abundance is already here for some goods (energy, information, food). The people building alternatives to extraction already exist. The problem is they are invisible to each other.

## The Framework (short version)

Seven principles, each with a section in the main doc:

1. **Universal Sufficiency** - unconditional right to food, shelter, healthcare, education, participation
2. **Ecological Equilibrium** - economy within planetary limits
3. **Democratic Sovereignty** - power from people, not capital
4. **Common Ownership of the Commons** - land, water, air, energy, data, infrastructure belong to everyone
5. **Voluntary Contribution** - work not coerced
6. **Non-Violence** - transition through preparation and legitimacy, not rupture
7. **Transparency by Default** - everything visible unless there's a specific reason not to be

Each principle has a corresponding directory category. The map shows where organizations practicing each principle actually exist.

## What This Project Is Not

- Not a manifesto. It's a working document with honest "we don't know yet" sections.
- Not trying to coordinate the organizations in the directory. They don't work for Commonweave.
- Not claiming post-scarcity. Some goods are getting cheaper. Others (land, water, care) are persistently scarce. The framework treats them differently.
- Not a nonprofit, a DAO, or a company. It's an open-source project maintained by one person with help from whoever shows up.
